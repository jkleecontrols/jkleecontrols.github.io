My research focuses on the complex and dynamic challenges associated with the routing and scheduling of unmanned vehicles. This includes both aerial (UAVs) and ground (UGVs) vehicles, addressing key issues such as optimization, real-time decision making, and system integration.

Key Areas of Interest

  • Optimization Algorithms: Developing and implementing advanced algorithms for optimizing the routes and schedules of unmanned vehicles to enhance efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Real-Time Decision Making: Creating systems that can make real-time decisions to adapt to changing environments and mission parameters.
  • Autonomous Systems: Integrating routing and scheduling solutions with autonomous control systems to enable fully autonomous operations.
  • Multi-Vehicle Coordination: Investigating methods for coordinating multiple unmanned vehicles to work together on complex tasks and missions.
  • Data-Driven Approaches: Utilizing machine learning and data analytics to improve prediction, planning, and execution of unmanned vehicle operations.

Applications

The research has broad applications across various fields including:

  • Military and Defense: Enhancing the capabilities of unmanned vehicles for reconnaissance, surveillance, and logistics in defense operations.
  • Commercial Deliveries: Optimizing the delivery routes and schedules for UAVs and UGVs in commercial package delivery systems.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Deploying unmanned vehicles for environmental monitoring, disaster response, and resource management.
  • Urban Mobility: Integrating unmanned vehicles into urban transportation systems for efficient and sustainable urban mobility solutions.

Future Directions

Looking forward, my research aims to push the boundaries of what is possible with unmanned vehicle technology. This involves exploring new methodologies, enhancing existing systems, and collaborating with industry and academic partners to bring innovative solutions to real-world challenges.
