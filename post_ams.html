Building a Carbon Fiber Drone from Scratch

CATIA V5, Pixhawk, Ardupilot, Dynamic Control System

February, 2021 Jackie Lee

• Designed and produced a lightweight quadcopter inspired by the materials used in the DJI Inspire 2, funded by Korea Expressway Corporation after being selected as a Designated University Startup Club in 2020. This project aimed to push the boundaries of drone performance by optimizing for strength and weight efficiency.

The drone’s main plate was crafted by hand-layering carbon fiber and fiberglass, combining these materials to address the fundamental challenge of weight in electric drones. This approach allowed us to achieve a structure that is both lightweight and exceptionally durable, critical for enhancing flight performance and battery efficiency.

Developed the drone’s 3D model using CATIA V5, ensuring precise engineering design before moving to physical prototyping. We also created a Raspberry Pi drone and implemented its control system with MAVproxy, demonstrating advanced integration of hardware and software.

This video showcases the testing of our custom-coded flight control system and real-time TCP camera module. By integrating these components, we aimed to enhance situational awareness and control precision during flight, pushing the limits of what can be achieved with DIY drone technology.
