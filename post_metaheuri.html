Meta-Heuristic Approach for UAV-UGV Path Planning Under Battery Constraints

Path planning, UAV-UGV collaboration, Simulation

January, 2024 Jackie Lee

This study focuses on solving path planning and scheduling problems for multiple Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) under strict battery constraints. These UAVs collaborate with Unmanned Ground Vehicles (UGVs) capable of recharging them in the field. The problem is framed as a Team Orienteering Problem (TOP), which is NP-hard.

Research Objectives

The key objectives of the study are to maximize reward collection by coordinating UAVs and UGVs to visit specific nodes in a 20x20 km² area. The UAVs are tasked with visiting these nodes while ensuring that they remain within their battery limits, which adds complexity to the path planning.

From left to right: Given Terrain Data / Simulation Data / Battery Status of Each Vehicle

Key Challenges and Problem Formulation

Our approach aims to solve the **Team Orienteering Problem (TOP)** with the added complexity of UAV battery constraints. The UGV plays a crucial role in this problem, acting as a mobile recharging station that enables UAVs to extend their mission times. The mission spans a 72-hour schedule, where we evaluate the efficiency of the UAV-UGV team in maximizing rewards by visiting various nodes in the map.

Meta-Heuristic Approach

We propose a multi-step **meta-heuristic algorithm** to address the problem, which includes:

  • **Greedy Insertion Heuristic** - to initialize the path for UAVs and UGVs.
  • **Improvement Steps** - such as 2-point exchanges, one-point movement, and route optimization.
  • **Tabu Search Heuristic** - to avoid local optima and find better global solutions.
  • **Rearrangement and Reattachment** - to refine routes, ensuring UAV efficiency and maximizing team scores.

Simulation Results

The algorithm was tested in a 72-hour mission simulation. Results showed that the proposed approach significantly optimized the UAV-UGV path planning and scheduling, with clear improvements in node visits, battery management, and overall mission success. The UAV-UGV team was able to visit up to 38,962 targets within the simulation timeframe, demonstrating the algorithm's efficiency.

Lee, J. J., & Rathinam, S. (2024). A Meta-Heuristic Approach for an Aerial-Ground Vehicle Path Planning Problem. In AIAA SCITECH 2024 Forum (p. 0230).
