2019 Rocket Launch Competition

Manufacturing, Dynamic Control System

July, 2019 Jackie Lee

Participated in the 2019 National Universities Rocket Association (NURA) Rocket Launch Competition, where our team competed against other top engineering students from across the country. The competition challenged us to design, build, and launch a high-performance rocket, testing both our technical skills and our ability to work as a cohesive team.

I was responsible for designing a dynamic parachute deployment system that responds to the rocket's posture changes during flight. Utilizing an Arduino microcontroller and servomotor, the system was programmed to eject the parachute at the optimal moment, ensuring a safe and controlled descent. This innovation was a critical component of our overall design, enhancing the rocket's recovery and reusability.

The project required a strong understanding of aerodynamics, dynamic control systems, and hands-on manufacturing skills. Our team worked tirelessly to refine the rocket's design, and the successful launch was a testament to our combined efforts and dedication. This experience not only improved my technical skills but also strengthened my leadership and teamwork abilities in a high-pressure, competitive environment.
